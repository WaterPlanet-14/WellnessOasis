About

Inpatient Care: Hospitals are equipped to provide inpatient care, which includes medical, surgical, and specialized services. Patients who require constant monitoring, surgery, or other medical treatments can receive comprehensive care within a hospital setting.

Emergency Services: Hospitals have emergency departments that operate 24/7. These departments provide critical care to patients in emergencies, including trauma, heart attacks, strokes, and other life-threatening conditions.

Specialized Units: Many hospitals have specialized units such as neonatal intensive care units (NICUs), intensive care units (ICUs), and oncology units to cater to patients with specific medical needs.

Diagnostic Services: Hospitals offer a wide range of diagnostic services, including X-rays, MRIs, CT scans, and laboratory tests, to aid in the accurate diagnosis of medical conditions.

Rehabilitation Services: Many hospitals provide rehabilitation services such as physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy to help patients recover from injuries or surgeries.

Pharmacy Services: Hospitals have pharmacies where patients can fill their prescriptions and receive medication counseling from pharmacists.

Maternity and Pediatric Care: Hospitals often have maternity wards and pediatric units to provide care for expectant mothers, newborns, and children.

Mental Health Services: Some hospitals offer mental health services, including psychiatric units and counseling, to address mental health disorders and emotional well-being.
